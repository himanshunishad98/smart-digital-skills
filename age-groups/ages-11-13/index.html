Ages 11–13 Curriculum Overview

Practical tools that Class 6–8 students use immediately. Every topic is project-based with real school assignments as context.

📄

MS Office Suite

Word for school assignments, Excel for data tables and charts, PowerPoint for academic presentations — skills used every week in school.

  • ✔Word formatting & document design
  • ✔Excel tables, formulas & charts
  • ✔PowerPoint slide design & animation

AI Tools Introduction

Age-appropriate AI literacy — how ChatGPT and Gemini work, how to write effective prompts, ethical use for school, and when NOT to use AI.

  • ✔How LLMs & AI assistants work
  • ✔Writing effective AI prompts
  • ✔Ethical use & fact-checking AI output

Canva Design

Canva for school project posters, science fair displays, book report infographics, and creative social media safe for school environments.

  • ✔Canva interface & template library
  • ✔Poster, infographic & presentation design
  • ✔Typography, colour & layout principles

Cyber Safety

Critical digital literacy for the social media age — online privacy, recognising scams, cyberbullying, digital footprint, and safe account management.

  • ✔Strong passwords & 2FA
  • ✔Phishing, scams & fake news
  • ✔Digital footprint & online reputation
Program Timeline

6-Week Learning Roadmap

Week 1

MS Word Mastery

Document formatting, headings, tables, citations, inserting images — everything for school assignments and projects.

Week 2

MS Excel Basics

Spreadsheet creation, basic formulas (SUM, AVERAGE, IF), sorting, filtering, and making charts for school data projects.

Week 3

PowerPoint & Canva

Slide design principles, animations, transitions in PowerPoint. Creating professional-quality posters and infographics using Canva.

Week 4

AI Tools & Ethics

ChatGPT and Gemini introduction, writing effective prompts for research, homework assistance, creative writing — and ethical boundaries.

Week 5

Cyber Safety

Online privacy, password security, recognising phishing and scams, cyberbullying response, and building a safe digital footprint.

Week 6

Final Project & Certificate

Students create a complete digital school project using Word, Excel, PowerPoint & Canva. Certificate ceremony with parent attendance.

FAQs

Parents Ask About Ages 11–13

What computer skills should Class 6, 7, and 8 students learn? ▼

Class 6–8 students benefit most from practical MS Office skills (Word for assignments, Excel for data, PowerPoint for presentations), an introduction to AI tools like ChatGPT and Gemini, Canva for visual projects, and cyber safety knowledge. These skills directly improve academic performance and prepare students for Class 9–10 and beyond.

Will my child learn AI tools safely? ▼

Yes. Our AI tools module is designed for middle schoolers with strong emphasis on responsible use, fact-checking AI output, and understanding the limitations of AI. Students learn to use AI as a productivity helper, not as a shortcut that bypasses their own thinking. All AI sessions are instructor-supervised.

What program comes after Ages 11–13? ▼

Students who complete the Ages 11–13 program can progress to our Ages 14–16 Future-Ready Skills program — covering advanced Excel, AI productivity tools, data handling, and career readiness for Class 9–10 students.
