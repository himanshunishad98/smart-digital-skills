FAQs

Parents Also Ask

What is the right age to start learning computer skills? ▼

Children can start structured computer learning as early as age 8. At this age, they can learn typing basics, computer safety rules, and introductory block coding using Scratch. By ages 11–13, students are ready for MS Office, AI tools, and Canva. By 14–16, they can handle advanced Excel, data handling, and career-oriented digital skills.

Which age group is the most popular? ▼

Our Ages 11–13 program (Class 6–8) is our most popular enrollment group, accounting for over 70% of students. This is because middle school is the ideal time for students to develop practical digital skills that directly help with school projects, exams, and future academic pathways.

Can my child join if they have no prior computer knowledge? ▼

Yes, absolutely. All three age-group programs are designed to start from zero. Whether your child has never used a keyboard before or just needs structured guidance, SkillNest instructors ensure every student progresses at a comfortable pace within a supportive batch environment.

How long is each age group program? ▼

All programs run for 6 weeks of live online classes. Sessions are 1 hour per day on weekends (Saturday & Sunday) or weekdays depending on batch availability. After completing the program, students receive an official SkillNest Digital Skills Certificate.
