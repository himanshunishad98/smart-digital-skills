🤖 What is a good typing speed for kids?

For school students in Class 6 to 8, a touch typing speed of 25 to 35 Words Per Minute (WPM) with an accuracy score above 90% is considered good. Mastering typing eliminates keyboard hunt-and-peck habits, saving students up to 5 hours weekly on research reports, programming tasks, and school presentations.

What the WPM Score Means for Students

Keyboarding is the physical gateway to digital competence. Students who hunt-and-peck type spend double the cognitive energy formatting simple reports and code files compared to touch typists.

By raising a child's typing speed from 15 WPM to 35 WPM, they write school reports and Scratch blocks three times faster, reducing homework friction and leaving more time for creative design and logical thinking.

🐢 Under 20 WPM — Novice

The student is likely looking down at the keyboard, hunting for keys. Focus is on finding letters rather than the project's content.

🎯 20 to 35 WPM — Class Standard

The student understands key placements. They are ready to write Scratch variables, type notes, and format MS Word tables comfortably.

⚡ Over 35 WPM — Advanced Kid

The student has excellent muscle memory. They can easily draft project scripts, write structured prompts, and code programs fluently.

FAQ

Typing Test FAQ

Frequently asked questions about student typing speeds.

What is a good typing speed for middle school students? +

For middle school students (Class 6-8), a typing speed of 25 to 35 Words Per Minute (WPM) with over 90% accuracy is considered good. Regular practice helps students transition from hunting keys to touch typing.

Why should school students learn touch typing? +

Touch typing allows students to type without looking at the keyboard. This improves productivity, saves time during online school assignments, and allows them to focus on creative writing and logical coding.

How is WPM calculated in the typing test? +

WPM is calculated by dividing the number of correct characters typed by 5 (the standard word length), and then dividing that result by the time elapsed in minutes.

Does the test store my child's typing inputs? +

No. The typing speed test operates entirely client-side in your web browser. No text typed by the student is transmitted or stored on our servers.
